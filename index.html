The FED Journey begins here... the Journey to CONQUER FED!!

Today, i met the captain of my FED Journey. Mr Henry, my FED teacher! Week 2 (More HTML & Intro to CSS)

Welcome to the second week of my Journey to conquer FED!!

This week, I dived deeper into HTML semantic concepts and began the CSS conquest.

Today's venture began with Ice-Breakers and lucky students shared either 2 truths and one lie or our work ethics, and during this Ice-Breaker session, I learnt that I have a classmate who is allergic to both peanuts and prawns, is ambidextrous, and classmates who's work ethics are being accountable, openminded and strict.

After the Ice-Breaker session, Mr Henry taught how HTML5 is able to give semantic meaning to a webpage. To do so, we can utilise tags suchs as <headder>, <nav>, <article>, <section>, <footer> and <main>. While adding semantic meaning to a website does not affect the overall look of a website, it is important to incoperate semantic meaning to a website as it improves the website's accessiblility to users who rely on assistive technologies to use the web. And with that, we have concluded the introduction to HTML quest!!

Next, we moved on to the Introduction to CSS. As a start, Mr Henry taught how we could uniquely identify HTML elements using IDs, Classes and Pseudo-Classes. Then, he showed us how to add colour, different fonts and box modelling concepts on CSS.

Finally, with the knowledge learnt so far, multiple hurdles stood infront of us to test what we have learnt. The first hurdle was to add semantic meaning to a HTML site. The second hurdle, we were tasked to create a web development blog which includes a lists, h1, h2 and an img minimally. The third hurdle was to Style a webpage's header, h1 and h2 elements. And the last hurdle was to restyle a webpage's font using Google Fonts.

After a meticulously conquering each hurdle one at a time, I finally conquered them and concluded my 2nd Week's Venture!!
